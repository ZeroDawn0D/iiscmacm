    Open Positions


    Student Representative

    Responsibilities
    The Student Representative is required to manage the membership drive. The Student Representative acts as the point of contact between the students and the Chapter. Good Communication skills are a pre-requisite for this position.
    Tenure & Count
    Annual (July to July) & 2

    Logistics Co-ordinator

    Responsibilities
    The Logistics Co-ordinator manages logistics such as room permissions, cab bookings, catering, etc for a Chapter Event.
    Tenure & Count
    Annual (July to July) & 2

    Activity Co-ordinator (AC)

    Responsibilities
    Broadly responsible for managing the lifecycle of an assigned event. The co-ordinator is expected to, using the appropriate channels, publicize the event, manage logistics, gather and submit relevant statistics and finances. Being at the centre stage of organizing an event will equip candidates interested in playing a bigger role in the Chapter's operations with hands on experience.
    Tenure & Count
    Duration of the event & One AC per event

    Editor

    Responsibilities
    The Editor is required to draft any document that forms the mouthpiece of the Chapter, as well as, the Annual Reports. The Editor must actively manage the Chapter's newsletter.
    Tenure & Count
    Annual (July to July) & 2

    Web Design/Social Media Manager

    Responsibilities
    Maintaining the Chapter's Webpage and social media accounts. Applicants must have good web development skills.
    Tenure & Count
    Annual (July to July) & 1

    About Us

    The IISc ACM Student Chapter is a joint initiative by the research students from Department of Computational and Data Sciences (CDS) and Computer Science and Automation (CSA). The chapter aims to further communication channels with alumni from academia and industry, highlight and recognize research within the institute and collectively bridge gap between research and practice.
